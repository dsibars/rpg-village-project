# Party Composition & Expedition Strategy

> **The Matrix:** 4 hero slots. Physical vs. Magic vs. Hybrid. Sustain vs. Burst. Custom spells. Infinite combinations.

---

## The 4-Hero Expedition

Expeditions allow **up to 4 heroes**. This is where all our systems collide into actual strategy.

### Hero Archetypes

| Archetype | Resource | Role | Icon |
|-----------|----------|------|------|
| **Warrior** | Stamina | Sustained damage, tanking | ⚔️ |
| **Mage** | MP | Burst damage, healing, control | 🔮 |
| **Support Mage** | MP (conservative) | Healing, buffs, minimal offense | 💚 |
| **Nuke Mage** | MP (devastating) | One or two world-ending spells | 💥 |
| **Hybrid** | Stamina + MP | Burst window, then collapse | ✦ |

> A "Support Mage" is just a mage who built their Magic Circle around healing/buff Glyphs instead of damage. Same system, different composition.

## Core Compositions

### Composition 1: The Balanced Party

```
[Warrior] [Warrior] [Support Mage] [Nuke Mage]
   ⚔️        ⚔️          💚            💥
```

**Strategy:**
- **Warriors** grind down enemies every turn. Never run out of stamina.
- **Support Mage** heals wounded warriors, buffs defense, cleanses debuffs. Uses small, efficient spells.
- **Nuke Mage** saves MP for the boss or elite pack. One 19-slot spell when it matters.

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Handles any fight length
- ✅ Has burst for emergencies
- ✅ Has healing for sustain
- ✅ Forgiving — one hero dying doesn't wipe the party

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ No second burst if the nuke misses or the boss has phases
- ❌ Slow against trash mobs (warriors chip away while mage saves MP)

**Best For:** General expeditions, unknown difficulty, boss fights.

**Resource Management:**
- Warriors: Self-sustaining. No potions needed.
- Support Mage: Uses ~20 MP per heal. Can heal 5-6 times per expedition.
- Nuke Mage: Hoards MP. Uses one 120 MP spell, then basic attacks.

### Composition 2: The Deathball

```
[Warrior] [Warrior] [Warrior] [Warrior]
   ⚔️        ⚔️        ⚔️        ⚔️
```

**Strategy:**
- Four warriors. Infinite stamina. Grind everything to dust.
- No magic. No healing. Just violence.

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Infinite sustain. Never runs out of resources.
- ✅ Simple. No MP management. No spell choices.
- ✅ Excels in long fights and marathons.
- ✅ No reliance on MP potions.

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ No burst. A high-HP boss takes forever.
- ❌ No healing. HP lost is permanent until camp/village.
- ❌ Vulnerable to AoE enemies (all four take damage).
- ❌ No magic utility (no cleanse, no buffs, no elemental advantage).

**Best For:** Short expeditions, grinding trash mobs, players who hate resource management.

**The Fantasy:** *"We are four walls of muscle. We do not cast. We do not heal. We hit things until they stop moving."*

---

### Composition 3: The Glass Cannon

```
[Warrior/Tank] [Mage] [Mage] [Mage]
      🛡️          🔮      🔮      🔮
```

**Strategy:**
- One warrior tanks and distracts.
- Three mages unleash coordinated devastation.
- First turn: all three mages cast their biggest spells.
- If anything survives, the warrior holds the line while mages recover.

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Deletes most encounters in 1-2 turns.
- ✅ Three different spell compositions = versatility.
- ✅ One mage can heal while others nuke.

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ If the boss survives the opening salvo, the mages are useless for 5+ turns.
- ❌ Heavy reliance on MP potions.
- ❌ Warrior alone cannot sustain against multiple enemies.
- ❌ One unlucky resist and the entire strategy collapses.

**Best For:** Speedruns, known weak encounters, players who love high-risk burst.

**The Fantasy:** *"We are glass cannons. We do not miss."*

---

### Composition 4: The Paladin Core

```
[Hybrid] [Warrior] [Warrior] [Support Mage]
   ✦        ⚔️        ⚔️          💚
```

**Strategy:**
- **Hybrid** opens every hard fight with a Body Inscription skill. 2-3 turns of devastating damage.
- **Warriors** sustain while the hybrid recovers both bars.
- **Support Mage** keeps everyone alive.
- The hybrid is the "trump card" — used at the right moment.

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Hybrid burst + warrior sustain = best of both.
- ✅ Support mage enables the hybrid to be reckless.
- ✅ Flexible — can play slow or fast depending on the fight.

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ Hybrid is useless after 3 turns. If the fight lasts longer, it's 3v4.
- ❌ Building a hybrid requires enormous investment (Tier 7 magic + 12 skill points).
- ❌ Only 1-2 hybrids per playthrough. If this one dies, the composition falls apart.

**Best For:** Mid-to-late game, boss fights with clear "danger phases."

**The Fantasy:** *"I am the blade. They are the anvil. Together we are war."*

---

### Composition 5: The Double Tap

```
[Hybrid] [Hybrid] [Support Mage] [Warrior]
   ✦        ✦          💚          ⚔️
```

**Strategy:**
- Two hybrids. Alternate their burst windows.
- Hybrid A unloads on turns 1-3. Hybrid B conserves.
- Hybrid B unloads on turns 4-6. Hybrid A recovers.
- Support Mage keeps both alive. Warrior fills the gaps.

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Near-continuous burst coverage.
- ✅ Two different Body Circles = two different elemental types.
- ✅ Incredibly satisfying to execute.

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ Requires **two** hybrids. Enormous investment.
- ❌ Both hybrids are resource-hungry. Support mage can't keep up with healing if both get hit.
- ❌ Very squishy. Two hybrids + one support = low total HP/DEF.

**Best For:** Late game, prestige runs, showing off.

**The Fantasy:** *"Two living spells. One battlefield. Nothing survives the alternating fire."*

---

### Composition 6: The Spellweaver Squad

```
[Mage] [Mage] [Mage] [Mage]
   🔮      🔮      🔮      🔮
```

**Strategy:**
- Four mages. Each with a different Magic Circle focus.
- Mage 1: Healing and buffs.
- Mage 2: Fire damage.
- Mage 3: Ice control (slows, freezes).
- Mage 4: Lightning AoE.
- Rotate spells. Never let all four run dry at once.

**Strengths:**
- ✅ Maximum spell diversity.
- ✅ Can handle any enemy type (elemental matchups).
- ✅ Four custom spells = four player-crafted strategies.

**Weaknesses:**
- ❌ All four share the same resource (MP). One long fight = total party wipe.
- ❌ No frontline. Any enemy that reaches the back row is catastrophic.
- ❌ Requires more MP potions than exist in the game.

**Best For:** Novelty runs, players who love the Magic Circle system.

## The Combinatorial Explosion

### Custom Spells × Party Composition

With the Magic Circle system, each mage can create **custom spells**:
- **Heal Circle:** Core = Life, Ring = `+`, `+`, Extend, Extend, Cleanse, Cleanse → Mass heal + debuff cleanse
- **Firewall:** Core = Fire, Ring = `++`, `++`, `+`, AoE, Burn, Burn → Massive fire DoT
- **Frost Prison:** Core = Ice, Ring = `+`, `+`, `+`, Slow, Slow, Freeze → Control-focused

Now multiply by party composition:

| Party | Mage 1 Spell | Mage 2 Spell | Mage 3 Spell | Dynamic |
|-------|-------------|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 2W + 1S + 1N | Mass Heal | — | Meteor Strike | Heal sustains, nuke deletes |
| 1W + 3M | Shield Buff | Firewall | Frost Prison | Buff → Burn → Control chain |
| 1H + 2W + 1S | — | — | Regeneration Circle | Hybrid bursts, regen keeps warriors alive |

> **The player is not just picking heroes. They're composing a symphony of systems.**

## Encounter Design Matrix

| Encounter | Best Comp | Worst Comp | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----|
| **Trash mob horde (8 weak enemies)** | Glass Cannon (4M) | Deathball (4W) | Mages AoE everything. Warriors hit one at a time. |
| **Elite single target** | Balanced (2W+1S+1N) | Spellweaver (4M) | Needs burst + sustain. Pure mages run dry. |
| **Boss with phases** | Paladin Core (1H+2W+1S) | Glass Cannon (4M) | Mages unload on phase 1, then are useless for phases 2-4. |
| **Marathon dungeon (10 fights)** | Deathball (4W) | Glass Cannon (4M) | Warriors never rest. Mages need camp after fight 3. |
| **Boss + minions** | Balanced or Double Tap | Spellweaver (4M) | Need both AoE (minions) and single-target (boss). |
| **Time trial (speed run)** | Glass Cannon (4M) | Deathball (4W) | Mages end fights in 1 turn. Warriors take 5+. |

## Resource Consumables in Expeditions

### MP Potions

| Potion | Restores | Rarity | When to Use |
|--------|----------|--------|-------------|
| Lesser MP Potion | 30 MP | Common | Support mage between fights |
| MP Potion | 60 MP | Uncommon | Nuke mage after a big spell |
| Greater MP Potion | 120 MP | Rare | Emergency refill for hybrid or nuke |
| Elixir of Mana | Full MP | Very Rare | Boss fight only |

> **MP potions are the great equalizer.** A party with 10 MP potions can play like a deathball. A party with 0 must conserve every drop.

### Stamina Items

| Item | Effect | Rarity |
|------|--------|--------|
| Energy Ration | +20 STA, +5% STA regen for 3 turns | Common |
| Warrior's Brew | Full STA, +10% damage next 3 turns | Uncommon |

> **Stamina items are less critical** because stamina regens naturally. But they let warriors use high tiers more aggressively.

## The Meta-Game: Building Your Roster

The village recruits heroes over time. The player builds a **roster** (bench) and picks 4 for each expedition.

### Roster Example (Late Game)

| Hero | Archetype | Key Skill / Spell | Role |
|------|-----------|-------------------|------|
| Arthur | Warrior | Multiple Attack ×9 | Sustained DPS |
| Elara | Mage | 19-slot Frost Prison | Control / AoE |
| Borin | Warrior | Power Strike ×7 | Tank / single-target |
| Kira | Support Mage | 13-slot Mass Heal | Healer |
| Thorne | Hybrid | Body Inscription (Fire) | Burst DPS |
| Selene | Nuke Mage | 25-slot Apocalypse | Emergency delete |

**Expedition 1 (exploration):** Arthur + Borin + Kira + Elara (Balanced)
**Expedition 2 (boss hunt):** Arthur + Borin + Kira + Thorne (Paladin Core)
**Expedition 3 (speedrun):** Elara + Selene + Thorne + Arthur (Glass Cannon hybrid)

> **The player becomes a strategist, not just a fighter.** Which 4? Which spells? Which consumables? Every expedition is a new puzzle.

## Open Questions

1. **Should the player see enemy composition before picking their 4 heroes?** Or is it blind (more roguelike)?
2. **How many MP potions drop per expedition?** This dramatically changes the viability of mage-heavy parties.
3. **Should there be a "camp" mechanic?** Rest between fights to restore MP but risk random encounters?
4. **Can hybrids be support-focused?** E.g., a Body Inscription with Life Core that heals on physical hits?
5. **Should there be synergy bonuses?** E.g., two warriors with Shield Bash get +10% defense?
